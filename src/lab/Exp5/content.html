Theory

Introduction:

The dynamic range of floating point comes at the cost of lower precision and increased complexity over fixed point. Logarithmic number systems (LNS) provide a similar range and precision to floating point but may have some advantages in complexity over floating point for certain applications.
Given the same bit-widths, floating-point and LNS numbers provide similar representation ranges. However, the arithmetic implementations of the two are quite different. For floating point numbers, addition and subtraction are simple; multiplication and division are difficult, while exponential operations are even more difficult. In contrast, multiplication, division and exponential operations are easy for LNS, but addition and subtraction are extraordinarily difficult.

Log of A Number:

The conversion from floating point to LNS involves two steps that can be done in parallel. Input floating point contains three parts i.e. S, E, M and output LNS gives two parts i.e. E, M. Let the floating point number A can be defined as



Steps to convert floating point number in to LNS

Step1: Take the log of number; log2 (A) = log2 (2E x M)
Step2: By multiplication rule of log; log2 (A) = log2 (2E) + log2(M), take E without bias
Step3: By power rule of log; log2 (A) = E x log2 (2) + log2(M)
Step4: As log2 (2) = 1; log2 (A) = 1 + log2(M); take mantissa with hidden bit `1' i.e. 1.M
Step5: Find log2(M) from Look-up table (for log2 of numbers between 0-1)
Step6: Represent the log of number as addition of E +log2(M) as log2 (A) = E + log2(M)

Example 1: To calculate log of floating point number A = 32’h41CE0000 i.e. 25.75

Step 1: log2 (32'h41CE0000) = log2 (24x1.609375)
Step 2: log2 (32'h41CE0000) = log2 (24) + log2 (1.609375)
Step 3: log2 (32'h41CE0000) = 4 x log2 (2) + log2 (1.609375)
Step 4: log2 (32'h41CE0000) = 4 + log2 (1.609375)
Step 5: log2 (1.609375) = 0.68650052718 from Look up Table of log2
Step 6: log2 (32'h41CE0000) = 4 + 0.68650052718 = 4.68650052718

Antilog of A Number:

The anti-log is simply the inverse log calculation. The anti-logarithm is defined as,

antilog2(A)=2A

where, A is a consist of exponent (E) and mantissa (M) part i.e. A=E+M
Steps to find the antilog of a log number is given below

Step1: antilog2(A)=2A
Step2: antilog2(E+M)=2(E+M)
Step3: antilog2(E+M)=2(E) x 2(M); find antilog of M from Look-up table (antilog of number between 1 to 2)

Example 2: To calculate antilog of fixed point log number A = 2.5

Step 1: antilog2(2.5)=22.5
Step 2: antilog2(2+0.5)=2(2+0.5)
Step 3: antilog2(2+0.5)=22 x 20.5
Step 4: antilog2(2+0.5)=4 x antilog2(0.5)
Step 5: antilog2(0.5) = 1.4142135624 from Look up Table of antilog2
Step 6: antilog2(2.5) = 4 x 1.4142135624 = 5.6568542495

Division:

Division in LNS becomes subtraction due to the logarithmic property given below
log2(A ÷ B) = log2(A) - log2(B)

Consider A & B are the two floating point numbers. Steps to compute division of A & B are:
Step1: Find log2(A)
Step2: Find log2(B)
Step3: Find C=log2(A) - log2(B)
Step4: Find antilog2(C)
Step5: Give the sign of result as XOR between A & B i.e. A B
Step6: Convert answer in to floating point number using fixed to floating point converter

Square Root:

Let A be the number in oating point format. Steps to find square root of number A:
Step1: Find log2(A)
Step2: Right shift log of number calculated in step 1 i.e. C=log2(A) >> 1
Step3: Find antilog2(C)
Step4: Convert Antilog to floating point using fixed to floating point converter
